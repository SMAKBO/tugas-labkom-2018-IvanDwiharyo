Formaldehyde is a colorless gas with a strong pungent odor.The chemical formula for formaldehyde is CH2O .It has a melting point of -92 ℃ and a boiling point of -19.5 ℃. Formaldehyde is extremely polar and is soluble in water and ethanol. This solubility is consistent with a high dipole moment.


Formaldehyde is a highly reactive aldehyde gas formed by oxidation or incomplete combustion of hydrocarbons.Formaldehyde solution (formalin) is considered a hazardous compound, and its vapor toxic.Formaldehyde is a colorless poisonous gas synthesized by the oxidation of methanol and used as an antiseptic, disinfectant, histologic fixative, and general-purpose chemical reagent for laboratory applications. Formaldehyde is readily soluble in water and is commonly distributed as a 37% solution in water; formalin, a 10% solution of formaldehyde in water, is used as a disinfectant and to preserve biological specimens. Environmentally, formaldehyde may be found in the atmosphere, smoke from fires, automobile exhaust and cigarette smoke. Small amounts are produced during normal metabolic processes in most organisms, including humans.

Formaldehyde (systematic name methanal), is a naturally occurring organic compound with the formula CH2O (H-CHO). It is the simplest of the aldehydes (R-CHO). The common name of this substance comes from its similarity and relation to formic acid.
