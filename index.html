About Formaldehydes

Formaldehyde (systematic name methanal), is a naturally occurring organic compound with the formula CH2O (H-CHO). It is the simplest of the aldehydes (R-CHO). The common name of this substance comes from its similarity and relation to formic acid.

Origin

Formaldehyde is produced industrially by the catalytic oxidation of methanol. The most common catalysts are silver metal or a mixture of an iron and molybdenum or vanadium oxides.

More>>>>

Physical Properties

Formaldehyde is a colorless gas with a strong pungent odor.The chemical formula for formaldehyde is CH2O .It has a melting point of -92 ℃ and a boiling point of -19.5 ℃. Formaldehyde is extremely polar and is soluble in water and ethanol. This solubility is consistent with a high dipole moment.

Chemical Properties

Formaldehyde have the other names. Its Formic Aldehyde, Methanal, Methyl Aldehyde, and Methyl Oxide. Formaldehyde has a trade names are Formalin, Formol, Morbicid, or Veracur.

Usefulness in Life

Formaldehyde is an essential building block chemical in the production of hundreds of items that improve everyday life. Little, if any, formaldehyde remains in the final products that consumers use.
