Chemical Properties

Aldehyde or alkanal is an organic compound containing a functional group with the structure –CHO. –CHO known as carbonyl (a carbon double-bonded to oxygen). A carbonyl center with the carbon atom aslo bonded to hydrogen and to R group. Aldehyde as an alkyl named formyl group. One example of aldehyde is formaldehyde. Formaldehyde known as Formalin is the simplest of the aldehydes. The molecular formula is CH2O (H-CHO). The common name of this substance comes from similarity and relation to formic acid. formaldehyde have the other names. Its Formic Aldehyde, Methanal, Methyl Aldehyde, and Methyl Oxide. formaldehyde has a trade names are Formalin, Formol, Morbicid, or Veracur.
